Buying Guide: How to Choose a Sleep Supplement

1. Bioavailability matters more than the label

Magnesium is a perfect example. Magnesium oxide is cheap and widely used ‚Äî but your body absorbs only about 4% of it. Magnesium glycinate or bisglycinate absorbs at 80%+. Always check the specific form of the active ingredient, not just the name on the front of the bottle.

2. Less is more with melatonin

The standard 5‚Äì10mg melatonin dose sold in most pharmacies is 5‚Äì20x higher than what research shows is effective. A 0.5mg dose is often sufficient to shift your circadian rhythm. Higher doses don't work better ‚Äî they just increase the chance of grogginess and vivid dreams.

3. Timing is everything

Magnesium and L-Theanine: take 30‚Äì60 minutes before bed. Melatonin: take 1‚Äì2 hours before your target sleep time (not right before bed). Ashwagandha: take consistently at the same time daily ‚Äî morning or evening both work, but pick one and stick to it.

4. Consider stacking

The most effective protocol we found: magnesium glycinate (300‚Äì400mg) + L-Theanine (200mg), taken together 45 minutes before bed. This combination addresses both the physiological and neurological aspects of sleep onset. It's safe, affordable, and our testers rated it higher than any single supplement alone.

üö© Red Flags: What to Avoid

  • Proprietary blends ‚Äî if the label hides individual ingredient doses, you can't know if you're getting an effective amount of anything.
  • Melatonin above 2mg ‚Äî higher doses increase grogginess and can disrupt your natural melatonin production over time.
  • Artificial fillers and dyes ‚Äî titanium dioxide, artificial colors, and unnecessary binders add nothing and may cause sensitivities.
  • "Instant sleep" or "knockout" claims ‚Äî no supplement works like a sedative. Any product making these claims is either misleading or contains undisclosed ingredients.
  • Magnesium oxide as the primary form ‚Äî it's cheap filler. Look for glycinate, bisglycinate, or malate.
